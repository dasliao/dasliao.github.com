Learning Journal One: Mad Libs 2.0

Oct-17-2021

Filling the form is one of the basic tasks we do on a website, and there are thousands of designs out there. A good form design is not as easy as stacking the labels and text inputters. However, it is also not about adding all the features. After reading the article and compare to my previous experience, I realized that those rules do not limit the design of the form, although they are extremely helpful and useful. Also, a good-looking form doesn't mean a good user experience. A good form design is about the most suitable for that specific form, such as the best place a label, the best way to provide instructions, the best layout to reduce user overload, etc.

The best practice form design I like is Google Account Sign Up form. The form is simple but extremely well designed. It uses the inline label, but when the user starts to type, the label will shrink and move to the upper left corner. The input field border is highlighted as blue. I usually don't notice those details, but they are thing driven a good form filling experience.
